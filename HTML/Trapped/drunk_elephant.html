Drunk Elephant

by Abigail
. . . . . . * * * . . * S * . . * . * . . . . . . Shogi: 1

The Drunk Elephant moves one square in any direction, except backwards. This is a mirror of the movement of the Blind Tiger which moves one square in any direction except forwards.

Spiral

Square

The Drunk Elephant fills the entire board, but it does so in an unusual manner. It fills up the board in an expanding, roughly triangle shaped pattern. It adds three rows at the top, for each row at the right and left side of the triangle.

Diamond

The Drunk Elephant can move as the Blind Monkey. Hence, it visits all the squares on the board by just following the Spiral.

Wedge

Folded

The Drunk Elephant can move as the Flying Cock, causing the %%PIECE%% to follow the numbers.

Flat

On the Flat Wedge, the Drunk Elephant moves in the same way as the King does, filling the wedge row by row, going from right to left and left to right, with a small side step before reaching the left end of each row.
