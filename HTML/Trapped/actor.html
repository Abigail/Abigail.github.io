On the Square Spiral, the Actor takes a long time to get trapped, but eventually, it does get trapped after 3,700,894 steps.

On the Diamond Spiral, it's not clear whether the Actor will get trapped or not. It won't get trapped for its first three billion steps.

Almost half the moves of the Actor on the Diamond Spiral are one-square Bishop moves (aka Ferz moves). Overall, beside the one-square Bishop moves, the Actor mostly makes Camel and Knight moves. But it does make longer and longer Bishop moves; the longest is 40,919 squares in the first three billion steps.

Folded

On the Folded Wedge, the Actor fills the entire Wedge, using an interesting pattern. It fills the Wedge in a balanced way.

While the Actor uses all its 8 possible Knight moves on the Folded Wedge, it uses only 6 of its possible 8 Camel moves. And except for one three-square Bishop moves, all the Bishop moves it makes are one-square moves, aka Ferz moves.

Flat

On the Flat Wedge, the entire board is filled as well, using a different interesting pattern. The Actor has a slight bias to the right hand side of the board.

The Actor uses a limited set of moves on the Folded Wedge: three one-square Bishop moves, two different Knight moves (one of them only three times early one), and just two different Camel moves.
