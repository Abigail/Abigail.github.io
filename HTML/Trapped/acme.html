The Acme combines the movements of the Queen, and the Camel. That is, the Acme can either slide an unlimited number of unoccupied squares in each of the eight main directions (orthogonal and diagonal), or leap to a square which is three squares away in one orthogonal direction, and one square away in the other.

Spiral

Square

% ./trapped -m 5k -l s_sq —div r CFFWW % Box: [-1, 1] [-1, 1] +------+------+------+ | . | 1225 | . | +------+------+------+ | 1260 | * | 1255 | +------+------+------+ | . | 1260 | . | +------+------+------+

The Acme can move as a Wazir. This means that Acme will follow the Spiral, visiting all the squares in order.

As can be seen from the heatmap to the right, the Acme makes about the same number of steps in each of the four orthogonal directions.

Diamond

% ./trapped -m 5k -l s_d —div l FWW % Box: [-1, 1] [-1, 1] +------+------+------+ | 1275 | . | 1225 | +------+------+------+ | . | * | 50 | +------+------+------+ | 1225 | . | 1225 | +------+------+------+

The Acme can move as the Blind Monkey. Hence, it will follow the spiral and visit all the squares on the board.

The Acme will make mostly diagonal moves, roughly the same in each of the four diagonal directions, and a single step to the right for each "layer" of the spiral.

Wedge

Folded

% ./trapped -m 5k -l w_fo —div r CFFWW % Box: [-1, 0] [-1, 1] +------+------+------+ | 35 | . | 35 | +------+------+------+ | 2480 | * | 2450 | +------+------+------+

Since the Acme can move as the Flying Cock, it will do so, filling the entire board visiting all squares in value order.

The Acme will mostly move sideways, and make one step diagonally forward when reaching the edge of the board.

Flat

% ./trapped -h -m 10k -l w_fl —div l CFFWW % Box: [-1, 1] [-3, 3] +------+------+------+------+------+------+------+ | . | . | . | . | 76 | . | 1982 | +------+------+------+------+------+------+------+ | . | . | 4061 | * | . | 1931 | . | +------+------+------+------+------+------+------+ | 1925 | . | . | 25 | . | . | . | +------+------+------+------+------+------+------+

The Acme fills the Flat Wedge using a simple pattern, with a bias to the right hand side.

On the Flat Wedge, the Acme uses only 6 different moves: two Camel moves, one one-square Bishop move, and three different Rook moves: two one square, and one two square moves.
