Plodding Ox

by Abigail
. . A . . . . * . . . . * . . . * * * . . . S . . . * * * . . . * . . . . * . . . . A . . Shogi: 1

The Plodding Ox either moves one square diagonally (like a Ferz), or slides an unlimited amount of unoccupied squares orthogonally forward or backward (like a vertical Rook).

The movements of the Plodding Ox are the movements of the Side Flyer, but rotated by 90°.

Name Game(s) Info
Plodding Ox Taikyoku Shōgi This is a different piece than the Plodding Ox from Wa Shōgi.

Spiral

Square

Using a complicated pattern, on the Square Spiral, the Plodding Ox visits all the squares on the board.

Diamond

The Plodding Ox follows a clockwise square spiral, visiting all the squares on the board.

Wedge

Folded

On the Folded Wedge, the Plodding Ox uses a six step loop to move two squares diagonally forward. This gives it an escape velocity of \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}\).

Flat

On the Flat Wedge, the Plodding Ox visits the right most square of each row, giving it an escape velocity of \(\sqrt{2}\).
