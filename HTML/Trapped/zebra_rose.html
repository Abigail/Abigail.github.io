The Zebra Rose makes repeated Zebra steps, but unlike the Zebra Rider, the Zebra Rose turns on each step (the minimum turn possible). After 8 such steps, it is back at the square it started on. Given that it start in 8 directions, and move either clockwise or counter-clockwise, it can reach quite a number of squares in one move. (Each square of its in between steps need to be unoccupied though). On a large enough board, away from any borders, the Zebra Rose can reach 32 different squares, assuming all squares are unoccupied. It needs to board sized at least 21x21 to be able to reach 32 different squares.

Game Piece Info
Zebra Rose

Spiral

Square

On the Square Spiral, the Zebra Rose moves outward, keeping always a near square as the boundary of squares visited. It probably won't get trapped, and certainly doesn't do in its first 10,000 steps.

Diamond

On the Diamond Spiral, the Zebra Rose moves outward, keeping always a near, rotated, square as the boundary of squares visited. It probably won't get trapped, and certainly doesn't do in its first 10,000 steps.

Wedge

Folded

The Zebra Rose seems to fill the Folded Wedge completely, working on just a few rows at the time. It probably won't get trapped, and it certainly doesn't do so in its first 10,000 steps.

Flat

The Zebra Rose get trapped after a mere 86 steps.
