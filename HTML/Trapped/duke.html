The Duke either moves as a Knight, or as a Rook, but then only up to four squares. That is, the %%PIECE%% either slides up to four unoccupied squares in an orthogonal direction, or jumps to a square which is two away in one orthogonal direction, and one away in the other.

The Duke moves like a limited range Chancellor.

This is about the Duke in Scirocco and Typhoon. The Renniassance Chess. There is also a Duke in Renniassance Chess. The King-Knight compound is called Duke as well.

Game Piece Info
Scirocco; Typhoon Duke ☋Is promoted Priest; The King-Knight compound in Cobra Chess is named Duke as well.

Spiral

Square

The Duke can move as the Wazir, so it fill the board by following the Spiral.

Diamond

Since the Chancellor never takes a long Rook move, the Duke follows the same path as the Chancellor does, and it gets trapped after 1,563 steps.

Wedge

Folded

On the Folded Wedge, the Duke fills the entire board by going left to right, and right to left, bouncing between two successive rows. This is the same pattern the Chancellor follows.

Flat

On the Flat Wedge, the Duke also fills the entire board by going left to right, and right to left, bouncing between two successive rows, but in a slightly different pattern than on the Folded Wedge. This is the same pattern the Chancellor follows.
