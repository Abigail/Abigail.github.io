The Archbishop combines the movements of the Knight and Bishop.

The Knight-Bishop compound is used in many games, and it is known under several names, of which Archbishop, Cardinal and Princess are the most frequently used.

On the Square Spiral, the Archbishop follows an irregular pattern in a clockwise direction around the origin, getting trapped after 6,386 steps.

As can be seen from the heatmap on the right, the Archbishop mostly makes Knight moves; the Bishop moves it makes are mostly one or two squares — only a handful are 3, 4 or 5 squares.

The Archbishop takes a lot longer to get trapped on the Diamond Spiral, but eventually, it gets trapped on step 318,714.

As can be seen from the heatmap on the right, the Archbishop mostly makes Knight and one-step Bishop moves. But it does take longer Bishop moves compared to the Square Spiral.

Wedge

Folded

% ./trapped -m 100k -l w_fo —div l -z 5 FFN % Box: [-2, 1] [-2, 2] +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | . | 79 | . | 79 | . | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | 18881 | 6009 | . | 6008 | 19023 | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | . | . | * | . | . | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | 6164 | 18797 | . | 18796 | 6164 | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+

On the Folded Wedge, the Archbishop fills the board evenly. There will only be a handful of rows which have been partially visited. A few different patterns are used when the Archbishop goes from one side of the wedge to the other.

Of all the possible moves the Archbishop can take, it only uses 10 different moves: each of the four one-square {Bishop](bishop.html) moves, three times as often backwards as fourward; the four mostly horizontal Knight moves, three times as often forwards as backwards, and, very rarely, a mostly forward Knight move.

Flat

% ./trapped -m 100k -l w_fl —div r -z 5 FFN % Box: [-2, 3] [-3, 2] +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | . | . | . | . | 23 | . | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | . | 2591 | 7479 | . | 12911 | 27370 | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | . | . | . | * | . | . | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | . | 26150 | 14975 | . | 3556 | 4626 | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | . | 96 | 10 | . | 58 | 22 | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+ | 133 | . | . | . | . | . | +-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+-------+

On the Flat Wedge, the Archbishop also fills the entire board, but it has a strong bias to the right hand side of the Wedge. The distance between the highest completed row, and the lowest unvisited row will always be increasing.

The Archbishop doesn't use all of its possible moves. 7 of the 8 possible Knight moves are used, but some far more frequently than others. Lots of one square Bishop moves, and a very few 2 or 3 square Bishop moves.
