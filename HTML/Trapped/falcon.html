Falcon

by Abigail

Falcon-Hunter Chess is a chess variant invented by Karl Schultz in 1943.

It introduces two new pieces, the Falcon, and the Hunter.

The Falcon moves forward like the Chess Bishop, and backwards like a Chess Rook.

Despite its moves being a mirror image of the Hunter, the Falcon doesn't get trapped. It will visit 25% of the squares, in a somewhat interesting pattern.

In Tori Shōgi, there is a different piece with the same name.
