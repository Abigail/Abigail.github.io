%%PIECE%%

by Abigail

The %%PIECE%% can slide an unlimited number of squares in each of the four diagonal directions, as long as the squares it slides over are not occupied.

Due to its diagonal movement, the %%PIECE%% is colourboundA colourbound piece will always stay on a square of the same colour: if it starts on a black square, it will always remain on a black square; if it starts on a white square, it will always remain on a white square.

Spiral

On the Spiral, the %%PIECE%% moves in a regular pattern. However, despite the possible moves of the %%PIECE%% being four fold symmetric, the resulting pattern isn't, making the %%PIECE%% slightly interesting.

If we take the limit, we see that the %%PIECE%% will visit \(\frac{1}{2} \cdot (\frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{3}{4} + \frac{1}{4}) = \frac{5}{16} = 31.25 \%\) of the squares on the board.

Wedge

Folded

On the Folded Wedge, the %%PIECE%% quickly escapes to infinity, moving in a stepwise pattern.

Flat

On the Flat Wedge, the %%PIECE%% escapes as well, keep to the right edge of the Wedge.
