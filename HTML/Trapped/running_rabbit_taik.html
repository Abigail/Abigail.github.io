Running Rabbit

by Abigail
A . . . A . . . A . * . . * . . * . . . * . * . * . . . . . * * * . . . . . . . S . . . . . . . * * * . . . . . . . . . . . . Shogi: 1

The Running Rabbit either slides an unlimited number of unoccupied squares diagonally forward (like a forward Bishop), slides an unlimited number of unoccupied squares orthogonally forward (like a forward Rook), or steps one square backward, either diagonally (like a backward Ferz), or orthogonally (like a backward Wazir).

Name Game(s) Info
Running Rabbit Taikyoku Shōgi This is a different piece than the Running Rabbit from Wa Shōgi.

Spiral

Square

The Running Rabbit can move like the Wazir, so it visits all the squares on the board.

Diamond

On the Diamond Spiral, the Running Rabbit uses a complicated pattern to visit \(75\%\) of the squares on the board.

Wedge

Folded

The Running Rabbit uses a six step loop to move two squares diagonally forward, giving it an escape velocity of \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}\).

Flat

On the Flat Wedge, the Running Rabbit visits the right most square on each row. This gives it an escape velocity of \(\sqrt{2}\).
