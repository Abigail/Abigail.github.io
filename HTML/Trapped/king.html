King

by Abigail

The King (♔) can step one square away in all eight directions.

The King is a Royal Piece, which means that if it's captured, that side loses. Of course, for our investigation, Royalness plays no part.

The King appears in many Chess variants, sometimes by different names.

In Shōgi (将棋) the King is also called the King General (王將) (for one side) and the Jeweled General (玉將) (for the other side).

In Tori Shōgi, the King is known as the Phoenix (鵬).

In the Shōgi variant Dōbutsu shōgi, the King is known as the Lion.

Scirocco has the Commoner, which moves exactly as the King, but which is not Royal. The Commoner promotes to a Wildebeest.

Other names for this piece include: include Mann (not Royal), and Prince (sometimes Royal).

Spiral

The King can move as the Wazir, and the Wazir will follow the Spiral. Hence, the King will visit all the squares on the board.

Wedge

Folded

The King can move as the Flying Cock, and the Flying Cock will follow the Folded Wedge. Hence, the King will visit all the squares on the board.

Flat

On the Flat Wedge, the King fills the board row by row, alternating going with and against the numbers, with a small twist just before reaching the left hand side of the Wedge.
