%%PIECE%%

by Abigail

The %%PIECE%% is a (2,2)-Leaper. On each step, it jumps to a square which is two away diagonally. It can jump over an occupied square. This makes the %%PIECE%% colourboundA colourbound piece will always stay on a square of the same colour: if it starts on a black square, it will always remain on a black square; if it starts on a white square, it will always remain on a white square.

The %%PIECE%% is more limited than just being colourboundA colourbound piece will always stay on a square of the same colour: if it starts on a black square, it will always remain on a black square; if it starts on a white square, it will always remain on a white square. If we give its starting square the coordinates \((0, 0)\), then the only squares it can reach are the squares \((x, y)\) such that \(x\) is even, \(y\) is even, and \(x + y\) is a multiple of \(4\).

Spiral

On the Spiral, the %%PIECE%% moves in a similar fashion as the Ferz, except that it's path is blown up by a factor two. It will visit all the squares of the board which satisfy the condition above. As such, the %%PIECE%% visits \(12.5\%\) of the board.

Wedge

On both the Folded and Flat Wedge, the %%PIECE%% escapes to infinity by visiting the right most square of each second row.
