Alfil

by Abigail

The Alfil is a (2,2)-leaper. On each step, it jumps to a square which is two away diagonally. It can jump over an occupied square. This makes the Alfil colourboundA colourbound piece will always stay on a square of the same colour: if it starts on a black square, it will always remain on a black square; if it starts on a white square, it will always remain on a white square.

The Alfil is more limited than just being colourboundA colourbound piece will always stay on a square of the same colour: if it starts on a black square, it will always remain on a black square; if it starts on a white square, it will always remain on a white square. If we give its starting square the coordinates \((0, 0)\), then the only squares it can reach are the squares \((x, y)\) such that \(x\) is even, \(y\) is even, and \(x + y\) is a multiple of \(4\).

The name Alfil is derived from the Persian word for Elephant, and the piece appeared in the game of Shatranj as the Elephant. Hence the depiction of the Alfil as an elephant. The proposed Unicode symbol for the Alfil looks like an elephant.

There are several pieces which move like the Alfil.

Spiral

Square

On the Square Spiral, the Alfil moves in a similar fashion as the Ferz, except that it's path is blown up by a factor two. It will visit all the squares of the board which satisfy the condition above. As such, the Alfil visits \(12.5\%\) of the board.

Diamond

On the Diamond Spiral, the Alfil moves in the same way as on the Square Spiral, visiting \(12.5\%\) of the board.

Wedge

On both the Folded and Flat Wedge, the Alfil escapes to infinity by visiting the right most square of each second row. This means an escape velocity of \(2 \sqrt{2}\).
