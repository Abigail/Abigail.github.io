Pawn ♙

by Abigail

The Pawns movement is very limited. It can only move one square forward (except the first move, where it has the option to move two squares forward, although some Chess Variants don't allow the two squares on the first move). Since we won't have other pieces on the board, it cannot move diagonally to capture. Hence, the Pawn will always move forward, never getting trapped; it just quickly escapes to infinity in a straight line, regardless of the numbering used.
