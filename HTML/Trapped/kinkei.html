The Kinkei alternates moving like the Gold General and the (Shōgi) Knight. That is, on odd moves the Kinkei moves on square orthogonally or one square diagonally forward, and on even moves, it leaps to a square two squares forward and one square sideways.

Game Piece Info
Kyōto Shōgi Kinkei

Spiral

Square

On the Square Spiral, the Kinkei escapes to the upper right, by visiting two squares on each row and column (above and to the right of the origin). By using a two step loop to move one square the upper right, the escape velocity is \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\).

Diamond

On the Diamond Square, the Kinkei uses a four step loop to move two square upward, giving it an escape velocity of \(\frac{1}{2}\). Only two files will be visited.

Wedge

Folded

On the Folded Wedge, the Kinkei escapes straight ahead by visiting all (except one) of the squares of the file the origin is on, and all the squares on the file next to it. It uses a four step loop to move two squares upward, for an escape velocity of \(\frac{1}{2}\).

Flat

On the Flat Wedge, the Kinkei escapes to the upper right by visiting the two right most squares on each row. By using a two step loop to move one square to the upper right, the escape velocity is \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\).
