%%PIECE%%

by Abigail

The %%PIECE%% either moves an unlimited amount of unoccupied squares orthogonally forward or backward (like a vertical Rook), or an unlimited amount of unoccupied squares diagonally backward (like a backward Bishop).

The movements of the %%PIECE%% is the mirror image of the movements of the White Horse.

Spiral

Square

The %%PIECE%% will visit almost all squares in a wedge to the left of the origin. As such, in the limit, the %%PIECE%% will visit \(25\%\) of the squares on the board.

This mirrors the squares visited by the White Horse.

Diamond

The %%PIECE%% makes half a revolution around the origin, then escapes backward in a one step loop. The escape velocity is \(1\).

Wedge

Folded

On the Folded Wedge, the %%PIECE%% escapes to the upper left, by visiting the three left most squares of each row. A three step loop brings the piece one square upward and to the left, for an escape velocity of \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}\).

Flat

On the Folded Wedge, the %%PIECE%% escapes to the upper right, by visiting the three right most squares of each row. A three step loop brings the piece one square upward and to the right, for an escape velocity of \(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{3}\).
