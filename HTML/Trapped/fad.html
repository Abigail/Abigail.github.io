The FAD either jumps to a square one away diagonally (like the Ferz), or two away orthogonally (like the Dabbaba), or two away diagonally (like the Alfil). This makes the FAD colourboundA colourbound piece will always stay on a square of the same colour: if it starts on a black square, it will always remain on a black square; if it starts on a white square, it will always remain on a white square.

Game Piece Info
Chess with different armies FAD

It turns out that on the Spirals and the Wedges, the FAD never makes use of its Alfil move option, so it moves exactly in the same was as the Goat.

Spiral

Square

The FAD follows the spiral, but it visits only every second square.

Diamond

On the Diamond Spiral, the FAD moves like the Ferz, visiting half the squares on the board.

Wedge

Folded

The FAD follows the Folded Wedge, visiting every second square.

Flat

The FAD fills half the board, row by row, visiting every second square. Before reaching the left side of the board, a small side step is taken.
