The **Archbishop** combines the movements
of the [*Knight*](knight.html) and [*Bishop*](bishop.html).

The [*Knight*](knight.html)-[*Bishop*](bishop.html) compound
is used in many games, and it is known under several names,
of which **Archbishop**, **Cardinal** and **Princess** are the
most frequently used.

On the Square Spiral, the **Archbishop** follows an irregular pattern
in a clockwise direction around the origin, getting trapped after
6,386 steps.

As can be seen from the heatmap on the right, the **Archbishop** mostly
makes [*Knight*](knight.html) moves; the [*Bishop*](bishop.html) moves
it makes are mostly one or two squares -- only a handful are 3, 4 or 5 squares.

The **Archbishop** takes a lot longer to get trapped on
the Diamond Spiral, but eventually, it gets trapped on step 318,714.

As can be seen from the heatmap on the right, the **Archbishop** mostly
makes [*Knight*](knight.html) and one-step [*Bishop*](bishop.html) moves.
But it does take longer [*Bishop*](bishop.html) moves compared to the
Square Spiral.

#### Folded

On the Folded Wedge, the **Archbishop** fills the board evenly.
There will only be a handful of rows which have been partially 
visited. A few different patterns are used when the 
**Archbishop** goes from one side of the wedge to the other.

Of all the possible moves the **Archbishop** can take, it only uses 10
different moves: each of the four one-square {*Bishop*](bishop.html) moves,
three times as often backwards as fourward; the four mostly horizontal 
[*Knight*](knight.html) moves, three times as often forwards as backwards,
and, very rarely, a mostly forward [*Knight*](knight.html) move.

On the Flat Wedge, the **Archbishop** also fills the entire board,
but it has a strong bias to the right hand side of the Wedge.
The distance between the highest completed row, and the lowest
unvisited row will always be increasing.

The **Archbishop** doesn't use all of its possible moves. 7 of the 8
possible [*Knight*](knight.html) moves are used, but some far more
frequently than others. Lots of one square [*Bishop*](bishop.html)
moves, and a very few 2 or 3 square [*Bishop*](bishop.html) moves.
